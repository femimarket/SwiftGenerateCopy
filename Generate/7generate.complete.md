# Generate — what this screen is, in plain words

## The mission

People with a song they love want to see it. They want pictures, they want a video, they want their song to *feel* like a music video — not just sound like one.

The Generate screen is where that happens. By the time a user leaves it for the first time, they've made their first music video scene. They've learned how the whole thing works without reading a single instruction. The screen taught them by walking them through it.

## Who it's for

A first-time user who opens the app with a song already attached to their account. They don't know what's possible yet. They don't care about how it works under the hood. They just want to see something cool come out of their song.

## The problem it solves

Making a music video traditionally takes a camera, lighting, location, editing software, a learning curve, days or weeks, and money for crew. This screen replaces all of that with three taps and a few minutes of waiting.

You describe what you want in everyday language — *"sunset, neon signs, rainy street"* — the app makes it. You pick the ones you love. You tell the app to turn them into a video. Done.

## The journey

**1. A welcome moment.**
A short kinetic intro — quick cuts of sample pictures, a diagonal split of warm and cool colour, one line of text: *"Let's make a music video."* No reading required. Tap anywhere to move on.

**2. A "ready when you are" moment.**
One button: *Tap to begin.* The very first time, a quick honest breakdown of what each step will cost in credits — including the bandwidth fee we pass on because we're a solo team, not a giant that can eat that cost for you. Once you agree, we never bring it up the same way again.

**3. First pictures arrive.**
Three different picture options appear in a clean Photos-style grid. A small nudge points to one: *"Tap one you like — we'll change it for you."*

**4. Make it yours.**
Tap any picture. Type what you'd change in plain language: *"make it sunset"*, *"add fireworks"*, *"closer-up portrait"*. The app gives you three new takes based on that change. You're back at the grid, with more options than before.

**5. Save the keepers.**
A heart sits on every picture. Tap it to save. Saved pictures stick around across sessions — they're your library, your favourites, your way of decluttering noise. The next nudge says: *"Tap ♡ to save it."*

**6. Turn them into a video.**
Once you've saved at least one picture, a *Make Video* button lights up in the top corner. Tap → the grid becomes a selector. Saved pictures are tappable, the rest dim out. Pick up to three. Hit *Make*.

**7. Keep going while it cooks.**
Video generation takes about a minute. You don't sit and wait. The video shows up as a shimmering tile in the grid, and you're free to keep making pictures, saving more, queueing more videos. When it's ready, the tile turns into something you can play.

**8. Watch it.**
Tap any finished video to open it full-screen with sound. Standard playback controls, just like Photos.

**9. The final beat.**
Heart your first video and the screen quietly celebrates: *"You did it."* You've now learned how to make a music video scene. Next time you come back, the welcome is gone, the grid is your home, and you already know what to do.

## What success looks like

A user who:
- Made their first video without reading an instruction.
- Understands the difference between *saving* a picture (curating forever) and *picking* one (making something now).
- Walks away knowing what's possible.
- Comes back tomorrow knowing exactly what to do.

## What this screen is not

It's not a full video editor. It's a *scene maker*. One scene at a time. Building a whole music video from many scenes is what happens later, in a separate tab that doesn't exist yet — a timeline editor where you stitch your scenes together.

## What we're explicitly not doing yet

These are real ideas, just not for this release:

- **Casting your own face** into the generated pictures.
- **Editing the timeline** of a finished video.
- **Browsing what other creators have made** in the app.
- **Using the user's own song as the welcome score** instead of the generic intro.
- **Multi-take video generation** — making three videos in parallel from one selection.

Each of these would be a meaningful upgrade to what we've shipped, on its own schedule.

## The vibe

Dark, but vibrant. Magenta and electric blue. Editorial, not corporate. Closer to the credits sequence of a music video than to a productivity app. The screen is meant to *feel* like a music video — kinetic at the start, calm at the centre when you're making decisions, satisfying when something you made plays back at you.

If a first-time user thinks *"this is the most fun thing I've made on my phone in months"* — that's success.
