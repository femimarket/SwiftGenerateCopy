# Feed — what this screen is, in plain words

## The mission

People watch music videos to feel something the song couldn't carry alone. They want to be inside it. And when something hits, they want to say so — and have someone hear them say so.

The Feed screen is where that happens. By the time a viewer leaves, they've been inside a creator's world — read what the song is about, seen what other viewers felt, said their own piece. By the time the creator opens the app the next morning, real people have answered the question they posed.

## Who it's for

Two people at once.

A viewer who's just here to listen, but the song lands harder than expected, and they realize the artist actually asked them something. They want to answer.

A creator who's already shipped a song and wants to know if anyone caught what they were reaching for. They want a reply that isn't just a heart.

## The problem it solves

Music videos on every other platform live alone. You watch, you leave, the song forgets you. The creator wonders into silence. Likes pile up; nothing real comes back.

This screen turns that silence into a conversation. The song is the room. The creator's question is the prompt. The other viewers are in the room with you. The thread keeps growing after you leave.

## The journey

**The video takes over.** When you land on Listen, the phone becomes a music video. Cinema black bars top and bottom; the song fills everything between them. Nothing competes for your eye — no row of icons screaming for taps.

**The creator's name is up top.** Small, quiet — the song title, a one-line theme. Tap that line and a sheet rises with the creator's own notes about the song: the imagery, the mood they were chasing, the answers to questions other people have asked. Underneath the notes is a button to download the project file so you can remix it yourself.

**A heart on the right.** Tap once, you've shown love. Tap again and again — it tiers up, gets warmer, gets louder. The creator feels the weight of how hard you meant it.

**Then the song asks you something.** A red dot lights on the avatar at the top-left. The creator has a question about the song you just heard. Tap, and the comments thread opens. You see other viewers' answers first. People you don't know wrote things like *"this hit me harder than I expected"* — and somehow that makes you brave enough to add your own. You write. You send. A beat later, the creator likes yours. Small thing. Real thing.

**The threads stay.** The avatar leads to an inbox that keeps the conversation alive — every post the creator drops, every reply you left, all in one place. Tomorrow the creator will drop another question. The dot will be waiting.

## What success looks like

A viewer who:
- Felt the song twice — once when it played, again when someone else said the thing they couldn't quite put into words.
- Left a real comment instead of just a heart.
- Came back the next day because they remembered there was somewhere their voice landed.

A creator who:
- Opened the app and saw their viewers actually answered.
- Knew which moment of the song hit hardest from what the room said.
- Sold a download to someone who wanted to make it their own.

## What this screen is not

It's not a feed in the swipe-to-next sense. There's no infinite scroll. You don't bail halfway through one song to chase dopamine in the next. Each song is its own world, watched on its own terms.

It's not a TikTok carousel of unrelated clips competing for your thumb. It's not an Apple Music album page either. It's the moment between hearing a song for the first time and deciding it matters.

## What we're explicitly not doing yet

These are real ideas, just not for this release:

- **Direct messages with the creator.** The threads inbox is built to scale into this; a row in the future is "message the creator" sitting next to "the creator's latest post."
- **Discovery between songs.** The next song is meant to be picked by viewer intent — a colour, a mood, an image — not by an algorithm we don't trust. We'll wire that selector later.
- **Likes-as-credits routing.** The intensity tiers are real; the credit accounting that turns a heart into something the creator earns comes next.
- **Stripe Connect** for creators to take that money out.
- **The viewer's own photo** replacing the gradient avatar. The slot is already there; it just needs to swap in.

## The vibe

Dark, but the song's own colours bleed across the screen — the palette shifts with the music. Cinema bars frame the video; nothing else competes. The chrome stays silent until the creator asks something or you reach for a tap.

It feels like watching a music video on a friend's phone in the dark — not like opening a social app. When the comment thread loads and you see other viewers' words next to yours, the silence breaks just enough for the song to feel like a place.

If a viewer leaves and says *"I felt like the artist heard me"* — that's success.
